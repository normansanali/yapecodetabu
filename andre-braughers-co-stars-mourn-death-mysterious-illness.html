Andre Braughers Co-Stars Mourn Him After Shocking Death From Mysterious Illness

Highlights

  • Emmy-winning actor Andre Braugher has passed away at 61, leaving behind a profound impact on his former co-stars and colleagues.
  • Braugher's career spanned over a decade, with notable roles in "Glory," "Homicide: Life on the Street," and "Brooklyn 99."
  • Co-stars Terry Crews, Dirk Blocker, and Joel McKinnon Miller have expressed their deep condolences and shared heartfelt tributes to honor Braugher's talent and friendship. #nineine #RIPAndre

It’s not only fans who are mourning Emmy-award-winning actor Andre Braugher after his unexpected death at the age of 61. Many of the actor’s former co-stars have shared touching messages online, showing how many people Braugher touched through his work, which included roles on Homicide and 99.

Andre Braugher’s Death Came After A Short, Mysterious Illness

Andre’s publicist Jennifer Allen confirmed his death to the media. While an official cause of death hasn’t been revealed, Allen explained that Andre was suffering from a “short illness,” though she didn’t specify further.

Braugher’s breakthrough role is widely considered to be 1998’s Glory. He starred opposite Morgan Freeman and Denzel Washington, who won an Oscar for the movie.

Andre went on to play Detective Frank Pembleton on Homicide: Life on the Street for seven seasons, even earning an Emmy for the iconic role in 1998. He would go on to be nominated for 11 Emmys over his lifetime.

He later had an equally famous television role with Brooklyn 99, which he stayed with for 8 seasons. With a more light-hearted tone than his previous projects, Andre acknowledged that Brooklyn 99 was a major shift in his career, one he was grateful for.

"I just felt as though it was an opportunity to do something strikingly different from the rest of my career," he said in 2019. "I like it because it just simply opens up my mind and forces me to think in a different way. So I think I've become much more sort of supple as an actor, and more open to the incredible number of possibilities of how to play a scene."

Andre Braugher’s “Brooklyn 99” Co-Stars Mourn His Death Online

With a decade-long career, there’s no denying Andre made his mark in Hollywood. Further evidence is the touching tributes his former co-stars and colleagues have publicly made in honor of him.

His Brooklyn 99 co-star Terry Crews made a poignant statement on Instagram. "Can’t believe you’re gone so soon," Crew captioned a tribute post to Andre.

"I’m honored to have known you, laughed with you, worked with you and shared 8 glorious years watching your irreplaceable talent. This hurts," he continued alongside a photo of Andre. "You left us too soon. You taught me so much. I will be forever grateful for the experience of knowing you. Thank you for your wisdom, your advice, your kindness and your friendship. Deepest condolences to your wife and family in this difficult time.”

Crews added, “You showed me what a life well lived looks like." He concluded his note with the show's favorite chant, "#nineine," and a note, "Rest In Peace, Andre. I love you, man."

Andre’s other Brooklyn 99 co-star Dirk Blocker released a similar message on social media, writing, "Fiercely intelligent, remarkably kind, supportive, generous and possessed a deep and extraordinary talent, and had even more to offer. I am devastated. I love him. The 9 years I was able to work with him and to just be in his presence was truly a blessing. My heartfelt condolences go out to his family."

Similarly, Joel McKinnon Miller, wrote in a post, "An actor prepares… Sending love to Andre’s family and friends and all of us who had the honor of working with him.”

Needless to say, Andre Braugher will be sorely missed by fans and friends alike.

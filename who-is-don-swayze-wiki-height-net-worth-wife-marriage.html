Swayze, Don Biography

Don Swayze was born in Houston, Texas, in the USA on August 10, 1958. His parents were Jesse Wayne Swayze, an engineering draftsman, and Patsy Swayze, a choreographer and dance instructor. Don was the middle child in the family. Vickie Lynn, an older sister, Patrick, an older brother, Sean Kyle, and Bora Song, a younger sister, are his siblings.

Additionally from an affluent family, Don. His paternal progenitor was an Englishman named John Swasey. John Swasey rode The Recovery during the Great Migration, arriving in Massachusetts.

Career

When Don Swayze was appearing in the 1980 movies Urban Cowboy, Father for Charlie, Shy People, and Edge of Honour as a homicidal lumberjack, he was able to pick up his dancing roots from his mother.

Don Swayze also enjoyed success in the television industry. He plays James (Jim) Mackey in the fifth season premiere of the Netflix series Longmire. Additionally, he made appearances in the soap operas Days of Our Lives and The Young and the Restless.

He has a recurring role as Gus in six episodes of the HBO drama True Blood in 2010. Don has made appearances in other television series, such as Murder She Wrote, The X Files, NYPD Blue, NCIS, and many others.

He also made an appearance in the television series Matlock, as an intellectually challenged murder suspect. In the western drama Paradise, he portrayed a member of a gang who held hostages in a motel.

He portrayed Jesse James in Lois & Clark: The New Adventures of Superman and Lucius in Charmed. He played Charles Hankel, the father and alter ego of a serial killer named Tobias Hankel, in a recurring role on the television series Criminal Minds.
